# Privacy

*Last updated: 16 July 2026*

Ans is built to be private. The short version: **the app collects nothing,
and this website uses only privacy-first, cookieless analytics.**

## This website

Privacy is baked into everything we do. This website uses Cloudflare's
privacy-first, **cookieless** analytics to count page visits and see which
pages are useful — that is the only measurement here. **We do not use
tracking cookies, we do not track you across the web, and we never collect
personal data.** There are no advertising pixels, no data brokers, and no
surveillance-style analytics; those are not welcome here. The count is
anonymous and aggregate, and you can verify it yourself: the only
third-party request the site makes is Cloudflare's small analytics beacon.

Everything below is about the **Ans app**, which goes further still and
collects nothing at all.

## What Ans stores

Ans keeps a small amount of data locally on your device so the app works the
way you left it:

- your settings (angle unit, display format, exact-result preference, haptics,
  display text size);
- the values and names of your variables;
- a small tally used only to decide whether to show Apple's rating prompt once
  — roughly how many calculations you have done and on how many separate days.
  Both stop counting at the point the decision is made, so Ans never knows more
  than "at least 20 calculations across at least 3 days", and it never records
  what you calculated.

This is stored in your device's local app storage (UserDefaults). It never
leaves your device, is never transmitted anywhere, and is deleted if you
delete the app.

## What Ans does not do

- **No accounts.** There is nothing to sign up for or log in to.
- **No advertising.** Ans contains no ads and no advertising frameworks.
- **No analytics.** Ans includes no analytics or measurement code of any kind.
- **No tracking.** Ans has no trackers and no third-party code at all.
- **No network use.** Ans itself makes no network connections and works
  entirely offline. You may once see Apple's standard rating prompt: iOS
  presents and handles it, and any rating you leave goes to the App Store, not
  to Ans. Ans sends and receives nothing.

## Widgets and Shortcuts

Ans provides Home Screen and Lock Screen widgets, a Control Centre control,
and an "Open Ans" shortcut. To display your latest answer and let you browse
recent calculations, Ans stores up to 12 completed expression-and-result pairs
in a private App Group container on your device. This information is available
only to the Ans app and its widget extension. It is never transmitted, used for
tracking or shared with another app or organisation.
