    How it keeps time

    Time is measured from clock timestamps, never counted in ticks, so a backgrounded or throttled tab stays exact — check back after five minutes and it hasn't drifted.

    If your machine sleeps mid-run, real time keeps counting, the way a physical stopwatch would in a closed drawer. When that happens the display adds a small SLEPT note showing how much of the run passed while asleep — already included in the total, not missing from it.

    The timer's completion sound is scheduled the moment you press Start, so it rings on time even from a background tab.
