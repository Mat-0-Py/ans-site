Assumptions

  • Motion is treated in one dimension. Choose one direction as positive and keep velocity, force, momentum and impulse signs consistent.
  • Mass is positive and constant; speeds are non-relativistic.
  • J = Ft uses a constant resultant force, or the average resultant force over the stated time interval.
  • The time interval is positive. Impulse and change in momentum can be negative when they act opposite to the chosen positive direction.
  • Calculations use SI units internally; the unit menus convert at the edges.

How it works

Momentum combines mass and velocity. It is a vector quantity, so in a one-dimensional GCSE question its sign records direction: an object moving in the negative direction has negative momentum.

Impulse is the change in momentum caused by a resultant force. For a constant force it is force × time; on a force–time graph it is the signed area under the graph. Select the matching relationship and leave the quantity you need blank.
