Assumptions

  • Specific heat capacity is constant over the temperature interval.
  • All stated energy is transferred to or from the sample; losses to surroundings and containers are neglected.
  • No change of state occurs during Q = mcΔT, and temperature stays constant during Q = mL.
  • Mass, c and L are positive. A negative ΔT represents cooling and gives a negative Q under the signed convention.

How it works

Specific heat capacity is the energy needed per kilogram for a one-kelvin temperature rise. Multiplying m, c and ΔT gives the sample's thermal energy change.

During melting, boiling or another change of state, energy changes the arrangement of particles rather than temperature. The energy magnitude is mass times the relevant specific latent heat.
